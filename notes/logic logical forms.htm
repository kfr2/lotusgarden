Barbara

  1. All A are B
  2. All B are C
  3. Therefore, all A are C
  • All Cows are ruminating animals.
  • All ruminating animals are docile.
  • All cows are docile.

Disjunctive Syllogism

  1. A or B
  2. Not A
  3. B
  • Max wears glasses or contact lenses.
  • Max does not wear classes.
  • Max wears contact lenses.

Modus Ponens

  1. If A then B
  2. A
  3. B
  • If it rains, then your car is wet.
  • It rains.
  • Your car is wet.

Modus Tollens

  1. If A then B
  2. Not B
  3. Not A
  • If it rains, then your car is wet.
  • Your car is not wet.
  • It does not rain.

Fallacies

Denying the Antecedent

  1. If A then B (A is the antecedent, B is the consequence)
  2. Not A
  3. Not B
  • If you study, then you pass.
  • You do not study.
  • You do not pass.

Affirming the Consequence

  1. If A then B
  2. B
  3. A
  • If you study, then you pass.
  • You pass.
  • You study.

Undistributed Middle

  1. All A are B
  2. All C are B
  3. All C are A
  • All ants are insects.
  • All beetles are insects.
  • All beetles are ants.
