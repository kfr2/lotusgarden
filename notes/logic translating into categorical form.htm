  • standard qualifier: "all," "no," or "some"
  • copula: connecting word -- "are," "are not"
  • subject and predicate terms that refer to categories

supplying group terms

  • All bachelors are unmarried => All bachelors are unmarried males.

supplying a copula

  • No seagulls like pecans. => No seagulls are birds that like penguins.

proper names and singular expressions

  • Socrates is a man => All persons identical to Socrates are men.
  • This fire fighter is due for a raise. => All persons identical to this fire fighter are persons due for a raise.

supplying quantifiers

  • Roses are red => All roses are red.
  • The ox is a strong beast => All oxen are strong beasts .* Emeralds are not cheap => No emeralds are cheap.

few, most, and many

  • Few people came to the game => Some people are people who came to the game; Some people are not people who came to the game.
  • similar for most and many

conditionals

  • "If... then"
    • If a man is a bachelor, then he is unmarried. => All bachelors are unmarried men.
    • If it rains, then the streets will be wet => All times it rains are times the streets will be wet.
  • "Only if"
    • A man is a bachelor only if he is unmarried => All bachelors are unmarried men.
  • "If and Only If"
    • will need to write two conditions
  • "only"
    • Only dogs bark => All dogs who bark are dogs.
  • "none but"
    • None but logicians love logic => All who love logic are logicians.
  • "the only"
    • The only people with keys are the coaches" => All people with keys are the coaches.
  • "all and only"
    • all logicians are lovers of logic => all lovers of logic are logician
