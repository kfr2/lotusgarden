traditional squares of opposition

  • tell us the logical relations between all 4 types of categorical statements
  • existential assumption -- whether or not we assume something exists. pertains to universal statements only.
  • example image
  • superalternates -- one implies the other (but not the other way)
    • a -> i (i does NOT -> a)
    • e -> o (o does NOT -> e)
  • subalternates -- falsity of one statement implies (but is not implied by) the falsity of another
    • false i -> false a
    • false o -> false e
    • falsity of the universal does not imply falsity of the particular
  • contraries -- both cannot be true (possible for both to be false)
    • A <--> E
  • subcontraries -- both cannot be false but both may be true
    • I <--> O
  • contradictions -- have exactly opposite truth values
    • A <--> O
    • E <--> I
  • assume a statement is true unless it says otherwise

modern squares of opposition

  • assume an item exists hypothetically
  • example image
  • is only concerned with contradictions: if statements are opposite (A->O, E->I), then the statement is valid
  • can't make an assumption from all to a particular thing
