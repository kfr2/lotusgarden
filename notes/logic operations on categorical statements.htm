conversion

  • converse of a standard-form categorical statement is formed by switching the subject and predicate terms
  • valid only for E and I statements

obversion

  • formed by:
    1. changing its quality (affirmative -> negative or vice-versa) 2deb. negating the predicate term
  • all forms are valid:
    1. All S are P => No S are non-P
    2. No S are P => All S are non-P
    3. Some S are P => Some S are not non-P
    4. Some S are not P => Some S are non-P

contraposition

  • formed by:
    1. switching the subject and predicate
    2. negating both terms
  • valid for A* and O** statements:
    1. All cats are mammals => All non-mammals are non-cats
    2. Some plants are not weeds => Some non-weeds are not non-plants.
