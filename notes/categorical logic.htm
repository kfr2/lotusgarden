Types of Logic:

  1. categorical logic
  2. symbolic logic

Categorical Logic

  • deals only with deductive arguments
  • categories are classes or groups of things
  • statements in categorical logic assert a relationship between one category and another

Category Examples

  1. Some dogs bark

  2. At least one member of the class of dogs is a member of the class of things that bark.

  3. All spiders have 8 legs

  4. All members of the class of spiders are members of the class of things having 8 legs

Categorical Forms

  1. A -- All S are P
  2. E -- No S are P
  3. I -- Some S are P
  4. O -- Some S are not P

Quantity of Categorical Statements

  • A -- Universal
  • E -- Universal
  • I -- Particular
  • O -- Particular

Quality of Categorical Statements

  • A -- Affirmative
  • E -- Affirmative
  • I -- Negative
  • O -- Negative
