"logic organizes our research and logic helps us assess the support for a statement"

empirical

  • those statements whose truth-value is determined by observations (or by providing evidence or hypotheses)
  • measurable

non-empirical

  • those statements whose truth-value is not determined by observations
  • statements that assert the conceptual
  • related to the unmeasurable

miscellanea

  1. truth-value is a property of statements
  2. an argument may be deductively valid then have false premises
  3. an argument may be deductively valid but not a good argument
  4. all statements of an argument may be true yet it is not deductively valid
  5. if the premises of a deductively valid argument are true, the conclusion is true
  6. if the conclusion of a deductively valid argument is false, at least one premise must be false
