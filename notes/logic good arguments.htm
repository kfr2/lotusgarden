good argument

an argument in which:

  1. given the premises, the conclusion follows from them either with deductive validity or inductive strength
  2. the premises are true

soundness

  • for a deductive argument to be sound, the argument must be valid, and the premise(s) and conclusion must be true

standards of strong inferential support

  1. deductive validity: given the premises, the conclusion follows necessarily
  2. inductive strength: given the premises, the conclusion follows probably (more likely true than false)

validity has nothing to do with the truth of a statement

ex: deductive validity

  1. All snakes are poisonous.
  2. The cobra is a snake.
  3. The cobra is poisonous. (necessarily)

ex: inductive strength.

  1. Most snakes are poisonous.
  2. The cobra is a snake.
  3. The cobra is poisonous. (probably)
